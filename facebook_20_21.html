Judge orders Facebook and Zuckerberg to turn over documents
NEW YORK (AP) - A New York judge says Facebook and its founder must release documents to a defense lawyer whose client has fled from criminal charges that he falsely claimed a majority ownership in the social media giant.
Manhattan federal Judge Vernon Broderick issued an order Friday against Facebook and Mark Zuckerberg.

U.S. District Judge Vernon Broderick ordered Facebook and Mark Zuckerberg to relinquish documents by Monday that were requested by Paul Ceglia's lawyer, Robert Ross Fogg.
The judge said he received a letter Thursday from lawyers for Facebook Inc. and Zuckerberg asking that an order he issued earlier in the week to promptly turn over requested documents be suspended until Ceglia is caught.
Ceglia's father told Broderick at a hearing last week that he believed his son might have fled because he believed Facebook and Zuckerberg were working together with prosecutors against him, jeopardizing his chance for a fair trial.
