Now tech powerhouse Facebook (FB) is entering the minimum wage fight, implementing new standards on benefits for its contractors and vendors.
Additionally, individuals who do not currently receive parental leave will be offered $4,000 in "new child" benefits, according to a blog posted on Facebook by COO Sheryl Sandberg.
"We are committed to providing a safe, fair work environment to everyone who helps Facebook connect the world," Sandberg wrote in the post.
While other tech companies have boosted benefits for some security guards and shuttle drivers, Facebook is among the first tech giants to publicly take a stance on the minimum wage.
Facebook's new benefits took effect May 1 for its largest vendors and support teams at the company's Menlo Park, California, headquarters.
