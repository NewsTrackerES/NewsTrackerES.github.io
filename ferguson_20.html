Despite calls for him to go, Knowles is the last man standing in Ferguson city government after a Justice Department report highly critical of the city's police force and court system prompted six officials to resign.
(AP) - In the days since the release of a Justice Department report that found widespread racial bias in the Ferguson Police Department, the mayor of the St. Louis suburb has become a part-time public servant in a full-time spotlight.
But he was little known outside the area until Aug. 9, when Officer Darren Wilson shot 18-year-old Michael Brown and Ferguson found itself suddenly in the national spotlight.
After the shooting, Knowles and Police Chief Tom Jackson became the public face of Ferguson, holding news conferences, hosting public forums and reaching out to civil rights leaders and protesters.
Knowles has a full-time job: general manager of the state-contracted motor vehicle license office in Ferguson.
