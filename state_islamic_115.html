Sunni tribes played a key role during the U.S. occupation fighting back al-Qaida in Iraq, the Islamic State group's predecessor, and their distrust of Baghdad's Shiite-led government eased the extremists' takeover last summer.
When the Islamic State group swept across a third of Iraq last summer, many Sunnis initially greeted them as liberators.
The few Sunni tribes that stood up against the Islamic State group have paid a heavy price.
With Tikrit taken, Sunni residents in Islamic State-held Anbar province, home to Fallujah, and Nineveh province, home to Mosul, Iraq's second-largest city, expect the Iraqi offensive to now turn its attention to their regions.
But many remain fearful of both the Islamic State group, as well as the Shiite militias involved in the Iraqi offensive.
