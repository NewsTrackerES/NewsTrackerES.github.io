Fighting between militias and Libyan Islamic State branch
SIRTE, Libya (AP) - Violent clashes erupted between Islamic State-affiliated militants and militias aligned with the Islamist self-declared government near Sirte in north-central Libya.
Field commander Mohammed Abdullah said that the fighting started when an Islamic State-affiliated suicide car bomber killed a solider from Battalion 166.
He said that they have information that the Islamic State affiliate has taken over a hotel, the main conference center and a prison inside Sirte.
The Libyan turmoil has provided fertile ground for militants allied with the Islamic State group, which is fighting in Iraq and Syria to expand its self-styled caliphate.
