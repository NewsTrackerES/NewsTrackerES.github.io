Japan confirms deaths of 3 nationals in Tunisia attack
Three Japanese were killed, and three others injured, in the militant attack on tourists in Tunisia, the Japanese government said Thursday.
U.S. first lady Michelle Obama, on a visit to Tokyo, said: "I want to express our condolences over the horrific events yesterday in Tunisia. Our hearts go out to the loved ones of those killed they are very much in our thoughts and prayers today."
Japan has confirmed the deaths of three of its nationals in the attack on tourists in Tunisia.
