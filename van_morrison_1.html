That number increased with Monday's release of Morrison's new album, "Duets: Re-Working the Catalogue."
Among his 16 duet partners: Mavis Staples, Taj Mahal, Michael Buble, Natalie Cole, the late Bobby Womack and Morrison's daughter, Shana Morrison.
For Morrison, "There were two parts to it," the 69-year-old artist told The Times in an exclusive email interview.
Morrison has spent the vast majority of his career looking ahead, writing and recording a string of studio albums that now numbers more than three dozen; "Duets: Re-Working the Catalogue" represents a rare dip into his past.
During that event, Morrison invited Staples, Womack and Cole to go into a recording studio to get the ball rolling.
"I first heard him in the '60s at a show with Aretha Franklin and Dr. John and I thought then, 'Who is this guy?'" Mahal told The Times at a performance Morrison gave last summer outside his hometown of Belfast.
"What I love about Van so much was that you couldn't categorize him," said Buble, who sings one of his favorite Morrison songs, "Real Real Gone," on the album.
Morrison's storied back catalog, which stretches back to his recordings with the Irish rock band Them in 1965, encompasses hundreds of songs that he and his duet partners drew from for the new album.
The album is produced by Morrison with Don Was and Bob Rock.
Most of the tracks were done with the singers together in the studio, though for a couple - including "These Are the Days" with Cole - scheduling required Morrison to lay down the basic track and his vocal, with his collaborator adding her or his part later.
As for singing with a musically idiosyncratic singer like Morrison, she said "it was just a matter of getting into his psyche and figuring out where he was going with his vocal.
